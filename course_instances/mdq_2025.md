- **Location:** Mar del Plata, Argentina

- **Audience:** Undergraduate and postgraduate students, researchers, and industry professionals

## Educational Focus
This edition emphasized the end-to-end workflow from ML model design to FPGA deployment, with a strong focus on:
- Model compression techniques (quantization, pruning, knowledge distillation)
- High-Level Synthesis for ML inference
- SoC/FPGA deployment
- System-level design considerations (power, signal integrity, EMC)

## Hardware and Tooling
- **FPGA platforms:** PYNQ-Z1/Z2, Zedboard, HyperFPGA
- **Toolchain:** Vivado HLS, Vivado, Python-based ML stack, PYNQ
- **Deployment scope:** Single-board FPGA inference (educational reference)

## Observations and Iteration Notes
- Participants benefited from early exposure to hardware constraints.
- Additional time was required for FPGA toolchain setup.
- The system was installed and distributed via a virtual machine.
- The course was constrained by the presence of a single instructor responsible for both hands-on activities and theoretical instruction.
- Future editions should include pre-configured environments and additional facilitators to better support hands-on sessions.
